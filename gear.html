Everything you need for off-road parenting in the Pacific Northwest.

The Essential Checklist

Transitioning from sidewalks to forest loam requires the right kit. We've compiled 20 must-have items for safety, comfort, and performance.

  • 1

    All-Terrain Jogger

    Look for air-filled tires (16"+ rear) and a lockable front swivel wheel for stability on gravel.

  • 3

    Deceleration Hand Brake

    Crucial for controlling momentum while running downhill with a 30lb+ stroller.

  • 4

    Rain Shield

    A custom-fit weather cover to keep the wind and drizzle off your passenger.

  • 5

    Portable Tire Pump

    Miniature bike pumps are lifesavers when you hit a sharp root and lose pressure.

  • 6

    Tire Sealant/Liners

    Pre-treat your tubes with sealant to automatically plug small punctures from thorns.

  • 7

    Handlebar Console

    Organize your phone, water, and keys for easy access without stopping your stride.

  • 8

    Stroller Fan/Heater

    Battery-operated climate control to keep the little one comfy regardless of the grade.

  • 9

    Reflective Tape

    Add high-vis strips to the stroller frame for those misty morning or twilight runs.

  • 10

    Insulated Bunting

    A "sleeping bag" for the stroller seat to keep legs warm in freezing mountain air.

  • 11

    Trail Running Shoes

    Deep lugs (5mm+) for you to maintain traction while pushing uphill on mud.

  • 12

    Hydration Vest

    Better than bottles in the console for you; keeps weight balanced on your torso.

  • 13

    Compact First Aid Kit

    Specific for both adult trail scrapes and toddler needs (antiseptic, fun bandaids).

  • 14

    Diaper Change Mat

    A foldable, waterproof mat for trailside changes on damp forest floors.

  • 15

    Collapsible Snack Cup

    Keep the passenger fed with spill-proof containers that fit in the stroller tray.

  • 16

    Sun Shade Extension

    Standard canopies often fall short; add a mesh extension for low-angle sun protection.

  • 17

    Headlamp (Stroller Mount)

    Clip a high-lumen light to the front axle to illuminate roots and rocks at night.

  • 18

    Dry Bags

    Stuff spare clothes and diapers in 5L dry bags inside the cargo basket to ensure they stay dry.

  • 19

    Bells/Noise Maker

    Bear bells or a small horn to alert other hikers when you’re approaching on narrow trails.

  • 20

    Stroller Lock

    A lightweight cable lock for when you want to park the stroller and hike a short "foot-only" spur.
